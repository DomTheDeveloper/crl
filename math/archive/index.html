OEIS A300997 — solved by Google DeepMind AlphaProof not ours

The stabilization time of a mass-splitting cellular automaton; the theorem a(n+1) − a(n) ∈ {1,2} was proved by DeepMind's AlphaProof (a complete, machine-checked Lean proof — 1,118 lines, no sorry). It appeared in the submitted package only as a computational reconstruction, explicitly “not a new priority claim”, so it lives here rather than in the catalog.

  • DeepMind_AlphaProof_a300997.lean — the proof (Apache-2.0, © 2026 Google LLC), mirrored from alphaproof-nexus-results.
  • CORROBORATION.md — our independent finite corroboration (reproduces the sequence and the gap lemma for n ≤ 600; verified in CI via tests/a300997.mjs). This is evidence, not a proof, and does not claim authorship.
  • OEIS A300997

By contrast, this project's own verified result — A317940 — has its full project site at /math/a317940/.
