Goldbach conjecture

Conditional reductionsUnsolved

The campaign isolated the real obstruction as a pointwise, lower-bound-compatible Type-II/Type-III estimate capable of separating primes from semiprimes—the sieve parity problem.

Concrete progress included:

  • the reflected correlation Σ Λ(n) μ(N−n) as a parity-breaking target;
  • a conditional 1+4/3 bridge reducing the residual factor structure;
  • identification of the critical factor geometry near (1/4,3/8,3/8) and modulus scale N^(5/8);
  • a rough-sieving criterion showing how a sufficiently strong weighted distribution theorem would force the residual factor to be prime.

Status: no unconditional estimate strong enough to close the bridge was proved. Goldbach remains open.

Checkerboard Dmono(22)

Exact finite frontier33 or 34 unresolved

The exact finite campaign established

33 ≤ Dmono(22) ≤ 34.

Boundary regimes and hundreds of canonical refinement cases were eliminated, no 34-point witness was found, and no terminal completed branch returned anything other than UNSAT. The remaining central symmetric cases are the hardest.

Status: no complete proof that 34 is impossible and no 34-point construction.
