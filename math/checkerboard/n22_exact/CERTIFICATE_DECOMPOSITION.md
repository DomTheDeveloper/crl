# Exact certificate decomposition for `D_mono(22) <= 33`

This document records the logical decomposition checked by the proof-producing
SAT workflows.  It is independent of solver heuristics.

## Ambient problem

The selected parity class contains 242 points.  A target-34 configuration is a
0/1 assignment with exactly 34 selected points and at most two selected points
on every Euclidean line containing at least three parity-class grid points.
The exact CNF additionally contains the proved weighted-slack identity; this is
a logically equivalent strengthening at cardinality 34, not an extra
assumption.

Every boundary side is itself a Euclidean line, so its occupancy is at most two.

## First exhaustive split

There are exactly two regimes.

1. **Deficient boundary:** at least one of the four boundary sides has occupancy
   at most one.
2. **All-double boundary:** every boundary side has occupancy exactly two.

These regimes are disjoint and exhaustive.

The dihedral symmetries generated by coordinate swap and 180-degree rotation
preserve the 22 by 22 grid, the chosen checkerboard parity class, cardinality,
and Euclidean collinearity.  They act transitively on the four sides.  Hence in
the deficient regime we may move a deficient side to the designated top side.
The `top-low` CNF therefore covers the entire deficient-boundary regime.  Its
Kissat DRAT proof has been replayed independently by `drat-trim`.

## All-double canonical top families

A two-point occupancy pattern on one boundary of 11 parity-class points is an
11-bit mask with exactly two set bits.  There are

```text
binomial(11,2) = 55
```

such masks; `n22_data.DOUBLES` lists all of them in increasing numeric order.

For an all-double configuration, choose the numerically smallest of its four
boundary masks.  A parity-preserving square symmetry moves a side attaining
that minimum to the designated top side.  If the resulting top mask is `t`,
every other boundary mask is numerically at least `t`.

`n22_exact_core.build_double(t)` asserts:

- exact target cardinality 34;
- every Euclidean line capacity;
- the exact weighted-slack identity;
- occupancy exactly two on all four sides;
- the top-side variables equal `t`;
- no other boundary side has a two-bit mask smaller than `t`.

Therefore the 55 CNFs

```text
build_double(t),  t in n22_data.DOUBLES
```

are a complete symmetry-reduced cover of the all-double regime.  They need not
be disjoint when several sides share the same minimal mask, but overlap does
not affect completeness or soundness.

Every successful matrix job stores:

- the exact DIMACS CNF;
- its manifest and SHA-256 digest;
- a textual Kissat DRAT proof;
- the independent `drat-trim` replay transcript;
- the compressed proof and its SHA-256 digest.

## Optional second-level partitions

For a slow canonical top family, fixing every variable on another boundary
partitions it into disjoint subfamilies.  For example, for top mask 24,
`emit_top_left_cnf.py` fixes the left mask `l`.  The canonical top constraints
already exclude `l < 24`, so the masks

```text
{l in n22_data.DOUBLES | l >= 24}
```

are an exact disjoint and exhaustive partition of `build_double(24)`.  The
coverage workflow reconstructs this list from `n22_data.py` rather than trusting
a handwritten count.

## Final implication

Once either

- every one of the 55 canonical top CNFs has a replayed UNSAT proof, or
- each unfinished top CNF is replaced by a complete replayed second-level
  partition,

the all-double regime is impossible.  Together with the replayed `top-low`
proof, no 34-point configuration exists.

The separately checked 33-point witness then gives

```text
D_mono(22) = 33.
```
