Let D_mono(n) be the largest number of points that can be chosen from one parity class of the n x n integer grid with no three collinear. Prellberg proved the elementary bound D_mono(n) <= 2n - 2 and recorded the sharper inequality D_mono(n) <= 2n - 4 for n >= 6 as a conjectural consequence of a boundary-forcing mechanism. We prove this inequality for every n >= 6. For all odd and even boards outside two finite exceptions, explicit nonnegative quadratic weights on rows, columns, and the two diagonal families of slopes +1 and -1 give constant coverage of every point in the prescribed parity class, while their exact capacity-two cost is strictly below the threshold required by 2n - 3 selected points. The thin colour of the 7 x 7 board is handled by a small integer line cover, and the two 6 x 6 colour classes by an exact finite Boolean verification. A complete Lean 4 formalization proves the all-n theorem in the genuine all-slope no-three-in-line model and includes an explicit axiom audit.
