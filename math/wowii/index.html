Conjecture 65

Exact Lean theorem provedCI passedUpstream candidate

Idea. Every nonempty target set in a connected graph is at minimum distance at most one. The minimum-degree and maximum-degree vertex sets are nonempty, so the two custom distance terms contribute at most two. Any nontrivial graph has a two-vertex induced forest, so the right-hand side is at least two.

distance contribution ≤ 1 + ceil(1/3) = 2 largestInducedForestSize G ≥ 2

Lean 4.27.0, pinned Formal Conjectures snapshot, no sorry, admit, native_decide, local axiom, or sorryAx. Axiom footprint: propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound.

Conjecture 143

Complete proofCompiledIndependent audit in progress

The proof establishes the target inequality through structural bounds connecting the graph invariant g(G), the tree parameter, and the graph's σ-invariant:

g(G) + 1 ≤ tree(G) · σ(G)

Conjecture 160

Repository statement falseSpecification caveat

A five-vertex graph—constructed as K2,3 plus one additional edge—violates the exact theorem currently encoded in Formal Conjectures. The counterexample works by evaluating every custom invariant in the Lean specification and forcing the claimed lower bound above what is possible on five vertices.

Important boundary: the result disproves the exact formal statement. The historical graph conjecture may have intended a different induced-cycle condition, so this is not automatically a disproof of every informal formulation.

No DeepMind disproof PR has yet been submitted.

Conjecture 316

Exact theorem compiledResearch-open proof candidate

Under the complement-average-degree hypothesis, a connected graph is shown to be complete, entirely pendant, or to have a non-pendant core that is a clique of size at most three. This makes the minimal total dominating sets classifiable by finite structural cases.

The full Lean development compiled against the exact official theorem: 8,055 build jobs passed; source guards and the transitive axiom audit were clean.

Conjecture 314

Paper proof candidateLean frontier

The structural campaign classifies connected triangle-free induced-P5-free graphs into chain-graph families or complete blow-ups of C5. The reduction stack and most family lemmas compile.

Remaining blocker: one global bridge lemma must package the classification into the exact top-level theorem. The comprehensive all-files check currently fails at that frontier, so this conjecture must not be labeled formally solved yet.
